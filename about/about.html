About Color Blindness

Definition and Causes

Understanding Color Vision

Color vision is the ability to perceive the different spectrums of light. This is done by light entering the eye and translated via the photoreceptors at the back of the eye. A normal human eye are sensitive to three different wavelengths of light. Hence why humans are known to have a trichomatric color vision.

Colour visions in humans can be split into nine categories. One of which is Trichromacy - those with normal visions. The other eight are known color vision blindness. Color vision blindness are either hereditary, can be caused by disease or injury.

Hereditary Color Blindess

It is possible for color blindness to be passed down from parents to their children. Red-green color blindness is more common in men. This does not mean that women are not susceptible to color blindness. Other categories of color blindness affects both male and female equally.

Color blindness due to disease or injury

Other than hereditary color blindness, a person can also get color blindness from diseases and injuries. Direct injuries to the eye or to the brain may cause a person to get color blindness. Some diseases such as glaucoma and multiple scelorosis thatt may cause harm to the eye directly or indirectly could also cause color blindness.
